About Me

I am a Senior at University of Pennsylvania pursuing a dual-degree between Systems Engineering and Physics. For the last three years I have been researching the applications of graph neural networks to energy systems, about which I published two papers. I believe that in many such systems we do not utilize all available information - for example most electricity consumers do not receive price signals. By taking advantage of new technologies and data we can dramatically increase system efficiency.

I am also a passionate member of Penn Electric Racing, where I am a software lead. We are a team of over 60 students dedicated to designing, manufacturing, and competing with a formula electric car at formula student (FSAE).

Publications

Damian Owerko, Fernando Gama, Alejandro Ribeiro
November 2019 ICASSP 2020

Optimal Power Flow Using Graph Neural Networks

In this paper, we propose using graph neural networks (which are localized, scalable parametrizations of network data) trained under the imitation learning framework to approximate a given optimal solution. While the optimal solution is costly, it is only required to be computed for network states in the training set. During test time, the GNN adequately learns how to compute the OPF solution. Numerical experiments are run on the IEEE-30 and IEEE-118 test cases.

Damian Owerko, Fernando Gama, Alejandro Ribeiro
November 2018 GlobalSIP 2018

Predicting Power Outages Using Graph Neural Networks

We deploy novel graph neural networks to adequately process weather measurements in order to determine the likelihood of a power outage. Tests on weather measurements taken in the region of New York City show a 1.04% error in the prediction.
